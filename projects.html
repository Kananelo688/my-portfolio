Predictive Admission Control and Bandwidth Allocation Scheme for 6G Networks

Supervisor: A/Prof Olabisi Falowo | Role:Principal Researcher

Project Description:
This project focused on designing a predictive call admission control and bandwidth allocation scheme for integrated terrestrial and non-terrestrial networks(6G) comprising of Low-Earth Orbit Satellite and Terrestrial 5G. The primary goal of the project was to develop admission scheme that reduces frequency of horizontal handoffs in satellite access networks, thus improving overall quality of service.

A Generalized Linear Model(GLM) was developed to predict call durations at the time of call initiation. Input features included user-specific details such as caller and callee IDs, call time, the available network resources, and satellite visibility times to admit users into the most appropriate network segment. Key metrics for evaluating the algorithm included call blocking probability and satellite handoff probability. Call Blocking Probability(CBP) is the probability that a call is blocked due to insufficient resources. On the other hand, Satellite handoff Probability(SHP) is the probability that a call admitted into the satellite access network experience a handoff- resulting from the current satellite moving out of view while the call is still on-going. Synthetic call detail records were generated for training and testing the model, ensuring realistic traffic patterns based on user behavior and network conditions.

This work demonstrates expertise in advanced network optimization, predictive modeling, and next-generation communication systems, showcasing innovative approaches enhancing the efficiency of integrated 6G networks.

Radio Access Technology(RAT) Selection Algorithm in Heterogeneous Wireless Networks

Supervisor: N/a (Course Project)| Role:Principal Researcher

Project Description:

This project focused on developing a Radio Access Technology (RAT) selection algorithm using the Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS) in heterogeneous wireless networks. The goal was to optimize the selection of RATs—such as 5G, WiFi, 4G and WiMAX—based on their performance in terms of bandwidth, latency, energy efficiency, cost, and reliability The algorithm leverages TOPSIS to evaluate multiple RATs by calculating their proximity to an ideal solution, considering various criteria such as cost, latency, bit error rate, and bandwidth. By integrating these factors, the algorithm ensures efficient and dynamic RAT selection, balancing QoS and resource utilization.

Key Components of the project included:
  • Developing a TOPSIS-based framework to evaluate and rank RATs
  • Implementing the algorithm to dynamically adapt to changing network conditions
  • Simulating the algorithm's performance under different scenarios to validate its effectiveness in enhancing network performance

This work highlights expertise in network optimization, decision-making algorithms, and heterogeneous network resource management, showcasing the ability to design intelligent solutions for improving experience in complex wireless environments.

Adaptive Channel Equaliation for Digital Communication Systems

Supervisor: N/a (Course Project)| Role:Embedded Systems Developer

Project Description:

This project involved designing and implementing a channel equalization system to address the challenges posed by channel distortion in digital communication systems. By employing the Least Mean Squares (LMS) algorithm, the system adaptively minimized errors and compensated for inter-symbol interference (ISI) and channel noise. The equalizer dynamically adjusted filter coefficients based on received signals, ensuring optimal performance in real-time scenarios.

Extensive simulations were conducted to test the system under various channel conditions and noise levels, with performance evaluated using bit error rate (BER) and convergence speed. This work highlights a strong understanding of adaptive filtering, digital signal processing, and communication system design, demonstrating the ability to develop robust solutions for enhancing signal integrity in modern networks.
